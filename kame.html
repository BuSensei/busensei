Animals like the turtle make up many of the 象形文字(shou-kei-mo-ji), or "picture characters." They represent a straight depiction of a creature, item or phenonenom, and were some of the earliest characters ever created.
The bone script character (left) began as simply a drawing of a turtle. The seal script (middle) form is streamlined, but still retains a distinct head, shell, and tail. The modern form (right) is further simplified, with parts of the turtle replaced with common elements to represent the headク body田 and tail乚.
